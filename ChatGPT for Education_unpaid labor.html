Polished Version

I woke up this morning to see OpenAI inviting “AI-curious student leaders” to join their ChatGPT Lab cohort. Let me say this clearly: at what point do we pause and ask whether this is collaboration or free labor dressed up as opportunity?

If a company with enormous resources wants research, campus insights, and product refinement, shouldn’t students be compensated for that work? Universities already struggle with unpaid internships and invisible labor. We should not normalize it further under the banner of innovation.

There is also a deeper concern. The educational ecosystem was profoundly disrupted before educators had meaningful time to reflect, prepare, or guide implementation. Now the same company is positioning itself as the guide for how AI should be used in classrooms. That feels backward.

Pedagogy should not trail product rollout like an afterthought. If I choose to integrate AI into my teaching, that decision must emerge from educational philosophy, research, and experience, not from marketing cycles or campus ambassador programs.

Innovation is not the problem. Lack of responsibility is. Students deserve protection, educators deserve respect, and education deserves more than experimentation at scale. Not the most pleasant Saturday morning read.

Students navigated uncertainty in real time. And now, after the shockwave, the same company steps forward to guide us on “how to use it better.”

Pedagogy should not be retrofitted around a released product. Educational philosophy should not trail technological rollout like documentation after deployment. If I integrate AI into my teaching, it will be grounded in research, developmental psychology, and intellectual responsibility. Not in corporate pilot programs designed to refine messaging and market positioning.

And yes, there is irony in asking an AI system to help articulate concerns about AI’s role in education. Perhaps that irony proves the point. The tool can assist. It should not define. Not the most inspiring way to start a Saturday.
